World Suicide Rate

Suicide is the act of taking one's own life. Attempted suicide or non-fatal suicide behaviour is self-injury with at least some deisre to end one's life that does not result in death. Every suicide is a tragedy that affects families, communities and entire countries and has long-lasting effects on the people left behind. Suicide does not occur in high-income countries, but is a global phenomenon on all regions of the world. In fact, over 77% of global suicides occurred in low-income and middle-income countries.

Overall Suicide Rate per 100k population across the World

Every year there are more than 800000 people taking their own life and there are many ppl who attempt suicide. Approximately 0.5% to 1.4% of people die by suicide , a mortality rate of 11.6 per 100,000 persons per year. Suicide resulted in 842,000 deaths in 2013 up from 712,000 deaths in 1990. Suicide rates have increased by 60% from the 1960s to 2012, with increase seen primarily in the developing world.

Average Suicide Rate across Age Group

In many countries , suicide rate is highest in the middle age or elderly. The absolute number of suicides however is greatest in those between 15 and 29 years old, due to the number of people in this age group. Worldwide, the average age of suicide is between age 30 and 49 for both men and women.

Suicide Rate across Gender

Globally as of 2012, death by suicide occurs about 2 times more offen in males than females. Males die 3 or 4 times more often by means of suicide than do females in Western world. China has one of the highest female suicide rates in the world and China is also the only country where suicide rate of female is higher than male.
